play

logline

When the leader of an artist residency kills the possum that’s been eating their cauliflower, the residents come to a shocking consensus.

the why

The world is shifting and we are moving towards a more equitable culture. However, many of us have become obsessed with not being canceled, eager for the simplicity of “us vs. them” thinking. We are interested in the potential for a productive way to deal with moral ambiguity — to normalize people we label “good” acting badly and “bad” people being good.

DAISY ROSATO

Director/Co-Producer

(she/her) Daisy is a queer, neurodivergent artist in Brooklyn NY. Raised in New Orleans, she’s drawn to creative work that rewards collaboration. She pulls from her Bard College BA in Theatre/German and work on film sets in the camera department. She moved to NYC in 2016 with a theatre company she cofounded, through which she premiered original work at BAX Upstart Fest and Ars Nova’s ANT Fest. As a writer, she has attended Austin Film Festival, Stowe Story Labs, and Studiofest VR. Storytelling is how she forms and shapes community.

a statement on the wga and sag strikes

We stand in solidarity with our fellow actors as they strike for fair contracts. The POSSUM set did not cross SAG picket lines. We’re excited to be a SAG project, as we believe in unions and how they support their members.
